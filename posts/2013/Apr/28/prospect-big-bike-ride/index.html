Prospect Big Bike Ride

Posted on 28 Apr 2013

The Dyfi Enduro is now a week away today it's been ages since I last did a real big ride. Therefore, I set out to plan a ride for this weekend and came across the Prospect Hospice Big Bike Ride by chance, a 50km ride over Swindon way. Ben came over on Saturday so we did a quick 25km ride round Boars Hill and also had a bit of a play on the Dean Court dirt jumps - they're better than I been expecting. Good news!

On Sunday we headed over to Swindon and signed on for the 50km loop, turns out that we had about 25 minutes to spare before the event started so had loads of time! The start was staggered into small groups but we didn't have to wait long. The first few km were on the croft trail which, from what we rode, seemed like pretty good fun. It was long until we (us and the guy in front of us) caught up to the group in front and had to go a bit slower but pretty soon we were on double track off the trail so could ride at our own pace.

Most of the ride was on double track with a few single track descents (and one climb) and a bit of road. We were pretty surprised by how hilly it was. There was even one road climb where, whilst we were going up it, I was the only person who stayed on the bike! It was really good fun and a nice day on the bikes. We were pushing quite hard to try to treat it as a bit of training for next weekend and it fit the bill pretty well! Unfortunately I did have a crash, about 100m from the finish line. There were two trees that we had to ride through and I found out the hard way that my bars didn't fit, oh well - no harm done :). Bring on the dyfi!
