Playing in the Dyfi

Posted on 11 Oct 2013

The Dyfi forest is always a highlight to any of my weeks or weekends. The riding is top notch. I designed a route that took in every (very nearly anyway) descent that I knew on the Corris side of the forest, including a bonus descent that I didn't know. We started off parking just below the climachx car park (it was closed) and span our way up toward Elephant. On the way up I spied footpath which I had thought had been logged so I decided that we'd throw this in as a bonus too.

The last time that I did footpath was quite a long time ago now, it was a pretty wide, quite muddy descent. Pretty average steepness for the dyfi. It's now a narrow, rocky descent but none-the-less still rather good. It is a bit of a short one though. Next, spin back up to Elephant!

Always a classic, starts out with a fairly long flat out as you dare blast down a rut before hitting a big bermed corner after which the trails unruts a bit and gets a bit rougher with a few small drops. We skipped the new Gravity Enduro but that they recently dug and popped out of the fireroad. We span along to the new bottom of Elephant (built for the enduro). This bit of trail is really, really awesome. They've added a descent that the dyfi really needed IMO. It's almost all dirt, unlike all of the descents which are very rocky. After the enduro the descent is now very rutty but that makes it a bit like riding on rails! I had to dab once going round a corner where my tyre scraped the inside of the rut but managed to clean the rest including the pretty steep and slick bottom section.

Next it was back up to the top of this bit to tackle Builth which was riding the same as it has done for years. Fast, rocky and pretty wild!

We span on up to the top of Dicko's which, again, is a Dyfi classic. With a mixture of rocks, mud and (since the gravity enduro) a bit of loam. As always, it was errr fast, rocky and pretty wild. Back up the climb past Builth and on Rocky Horror 1. I'd only done this once before and last time it was overgrown with brambles so I was prepared to have to do some clearing, only had to clear 1 bramble! The rest of the run was clean. It's steep and very, very rocky down this one. A few interesting bits where line choice matters quite a bit but yet another cracking descent.

Back up the climb past builth yet again and we did the short Dyfi Warm Up enduro descent. It's a bit of fun, not too steep but rocky with wheel grabbing holes if you pick your line wrong. We span back up past builth again (for the last time!) and cracked on to Nemesis. We found it quite a way up a technical singletrack climb. I hadn't done this before. Wow! It's great. It started out a bit twisty and very steep before developing out in a bit more of a flatout and then getting steep and tight again. Mixture of rocks and mud this time, mostly rocks! It dropped us out onto a fireroad a bit in the middle of nowhere so it was a bit of a trek back up to Tony. Despite this, Nemesis is getting added to permanent list of Dyfi trails that need riding.

We finished up on Tony the Tiger, the climachx final descent. Once again very rocky but also very long. Starts out in the woods and then opens up to where they've been logging before dropping back into the trees traversing back and forth along the hill before a load of tight(ish) switchbacks leading to the bottom. A nice way to finish out a ride!

Man, I love the Dyfi.
