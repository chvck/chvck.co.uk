KS Lev seatpost

Posted on 13 Mar 2013

I ordered one of those from Superstar yesterday and was pretty impressed when it turned up today. It feels like a nice piece of kit and is well finished. I also go free Haribo, yay! Anyway, on to the fitting, the instructions say to cut the outer into 2 pieces to enable you to fit a barrel adjuster, I chose not to bother and to see how it faired without it. Cutting the cables to size is slightly fiddly as the end piece has to be very exact and can't have cable sticking out. It took me a good while to get this bit right and get it fitted but wasn't too difficult. As a little bonus the lever can also act as a lock-on for an ODI compatible grip which is pretty cool.

The next step was the saddle, this is a lesson in why not to skip several pages of instructions as I wound up with my saddle pointing 90 degrees to where it should have been so I had to take it back off.

Turns out that the cradle for the saddle can be turned to various positions, bonus. To do this you have to take the head of the seatpost apart and unscrew the valve cap for the air - I took this opportunity to add a bit more air also. Once done I refitted the seatpost and wadda ya know, pointing in the correct direction.

It works well and feels smooth going through the travel. Thus far impressed - can't wait to take it on a ride.
