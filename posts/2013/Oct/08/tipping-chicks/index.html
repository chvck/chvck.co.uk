Tipping chicks

Posted on 08 Oct 2013

My Dad owns a chicken farm where he takes chicks from a couple of days old and grows them until they're about ready to start laying eggs. At the point where they're old enough to lay they get taken off to another farm and he then gets a new batch of chicks. As I'm currently on holiday and on the farm I got roped into "tipping chicks" today.

The process is basically that chicks arrive in stacked plastic baskets on trolleys in a lorry. The trolleys are rolled into the shed and we then put the baskets out on feed on the shed floor. Once this done someone sprays the chicks with a vaccine and we wait for them to dry. Once that's done we (gently!) tip them out onto the feed, stack up the empty baskets and roll the baskets out to be reloaded back on to the lorry. Simple!

Not so great when your body is aching from riding and the temperature in the shed is 27C though...
